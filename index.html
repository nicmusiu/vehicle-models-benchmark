A Comprehensive Benchmark of Vehicle Dynamics Models for Autonomous Racing: a Deep Dive into MPC

Nicola Musiu, Eugenio Mascaro, Ayoub Raji, Alessandro De Felice, Silvio Sorrentino, Marko Bertogna
University of Modena and Reggio Emilia

On board with Unimore Racing autonomous racecar EAV24 during testing in Yas Marina (south configuration)

Abstract

This work aims to benchmark vehicle dynamics models com- monly used in autonomous racing by presenting their structures, formu- lations, key parameters, and simplifying assumptions. We analyze the applicable ranges of these models by comparing the various solutions proposed in the literature. Their limitations are highlighted through sim- ulations, including both standard and real on-track maneuvers, where differences in model accuracy and predictive capability are evaluated. Additionally, a validated methodology for parameter identification is pre- sented. The study also explores model linearization and numerical stabil- ity, both of which are particularly relevant for control applications. In the first part of this work, through open-loop simulations, simplified models are evaluated against a high-fidelity multi-body model. The evaluation primarily focuses on vehicle dynamics accuracy and numerical stability of the common integrators. In the second part, the models are integrated into a Model Predictive Controller (MPC) to assess their performance in closed-loop path-tracking for an autonomous racecar. For this step, real-time simulations are employed, with the multi-body model serving as the vehicle’s physical representation. The MPC was also validated on real-world scenarios, using a Dallara EAV24 Super Formula car, in the Yas Marina circuit during the Abu Dhabi Autonomous Racing League (A2RL) testing.
